Before you leave...


How do we remember a place, a person, an object, a scent that no longer exists but only in our memories?

Growing up, my mom would always tell me stories recalling her childhood in the poor rural mountainous village of Falam, Myanmar where her dad worked as a doctor. Stories of the view above the clouds from on top of the mountain, her mom's garden, chasing fireflies, climbing trees to find bird nests, stumbling across ghosts and whispering spirits that wander the forests… places that she left behind decades ago and that no longer exists from the cross fire of bombs in an ongoing civil war…

Before You Leave is a narrative based video game that takes inspiration from these stories and is an attempt to retell some of these memories through the perspective of a village doctor reflecting on his life in the village while following a ghost. It's a diaspora of memories and layers of generational storytelling lost in translation from my grandpa to my mom and now to me.

While the player is invited to explore the story by prompting the game console through typing short commands or using arrow keys to navigate the character through the storybook-like pages of the browser, they are also able to directly participate and contribute to building a collective memory by being prompted throughout the game to answer questions from their own memory by either drawing or writing in the book provided with the game.

“Draw your childhood home.” “Describe your mom's voice.” “Draw the hands of someone you loved.”

Before You Leave is a dialogue between digital and physical, past and present, game and player, but most of all an invitation to remember.
